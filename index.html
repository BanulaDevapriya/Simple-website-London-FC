Elephants

Sri Lankan elephants are the largest subspecies reaching a shoulder height of between 2 and 3.5m (6.6 and 11.5 ft.), weigh between 2,000 and 5,500kg (4,400 and 12,100lb.), and have 19 pairs of ribs. Their skin color is darker than of indices and of Sumatrans with larger and more distinct patches of depigmentation on ears, face, trunk and belly.

Sri Lankan Monkeys

Many monkey species are tree-dwelling, although there are species that live primarily on the ground, such as baboons. Most species are mainly active during the day. Monkeys are generally considered to be intelligent, especially the Old World monkeys.
